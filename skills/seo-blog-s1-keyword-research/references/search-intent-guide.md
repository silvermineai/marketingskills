# Search Intent Classification Guide

How to classify search intent and determine blog fit for each keyword.

## The Four Intent Types

### Informational (Best Blog Fit)

The searcher wants to learn something.

**Signal words:** how, what, why, when, who, guide, tutorial, explained, examples, tips, ways to, steps

**SERP indicators:**
- Top results are blog posts, guides, or articles
- Featured snippets (paragraph, list, or table)
- "People Also Ask" boxes
- Knowledge panels

**Example keywords:**
- "how to set up Google Analytics 4"
- "what is marketing automation"
- "content marketing strategy examples"

### Commercial Investigation (Great Blog Fit)

The searcher is researching before buying.

**Signal words:** best, top, vs, comparison, review, alternative, recommended, pros and cons, worth it

**SERP indicators:**
- Listicles and comparison articles rank
- Review sites appear
- Some product pages mixed in
- Shopping ads may appear but don't dominate

**Example keywords:**
- "best email marketing tools for small business"
- "HubSpot vs Mailchimp"
- "is Ahrefs worth it"

### Transactional (Lower Blog Fit)

The searcher wants to buy or take action.

**Signal words:** buy, pricing, discount, coupon, deal, sign up, subscribe, order, purchase, cost

**SERP indicators:**
- Product pages dominate
- Shopping ads and PPC ads prominent
- Pricing pages rank
- Few or no blog posts in top 10

**Usually better as:** Landing pages, pricing pages, product pages

### Navigational (Skip)

The searcher wants a specific website or page.

**Signal words:** [brand name], login, sign in, app, download, official site, customer service

**Action:** Always skip these. You cannot outrank the brand itself.

## SERP Validation Process

Before committing to any keyword:

1. Google the keyword
2. Look at the **top 5 organic results** (ignore ads)
3. Ask: "Are the results mostly blog posts and articles?"
   - **Yes** → Strong blog fit, proceed
   - **Mixed** (some product pages, some blogs) → Possible, look at the blog results more closely
   - **No** (all product pages, Wikipedia, tools) → Poor blog fit, skip or reconsider angle

## Intent Overlap

Many keywords have mixed intent. When in doubt:

- If **any** blog posts rank in the top 5, it's worth targeting
- If the SERP shows a featured snippet from a blog, Google clearly rewards informational content for this query
- If the SERP is 100% product pages, a blog post almost certainly won't rank
