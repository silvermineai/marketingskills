# Compliance Checklist Reference

Detailed guidance for the final compliance scan before publishing.

## Content Safety Checks

### No Pricing or Cost Claims

**Flag and remove:**
- Specific dollar amounts ("starts at $99/month")
- Relative pricing ("cheaper than competitors")
- Cost comparisons ("saves you $X per year")
- ROI percentages with specific numbers ("300% ROI")

**Acceptable alternatives:**
- "Contact us for pricing"
- "Pricing varies based on your needs"
- "Many businesses report significant improvements in [metric]"

### No Guarantees or Promises

**Flag and remove:**
- "You will rank #1"
- "Guaranteed results"
- "We promise [outcome]"
- "This always works"
- Specific timelines for results ("rank in 30 days")

**Acceptable alternatives:**
- "In our experience, this approach tends to improve [metric]"
- "Many businesses see improvements when implementing [strategy]"
- "Results vary depending on [factors], but the principles are sound"

### No Medical, Legal, or Financial Advice

**Flag if the article:**
- Recommends specific medical treatments or supplements
- Gives legal advice ("you should sue" or "this is legal/illegal")
- Recommends specific financial investments or strategies
- Makes health claims about products

**If the topic borders on these areas:**
- Add disclaimers: "This is not [medical/legal/financial] advice. Consult a professional."
- Keep language educational, not prescriptive

### No Competitor Disparagement

**Flag and remove:**
- "[Competitor] is terrible at [thing]"
- "Unlike [Competitor], which fails to..."
- Negative characterizations of specific companies

**Acceptable alternatives:**
- Objective feature comparisons with factual data
- "Some tools focus on [X], while we prioritize [Y]"
- Let the reader draw their own conclusions from facts

### No Adult Content or Inappropriate Material

Self-explanatory. Also flag:
- Profanity or crude language
- Violent imagery
- Discriminatory language
- Content that could be offensive to any demographic

## AI Slop Detection

### Phrases to Flag and Remove

**Opening filler:**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In today's digital landscape..."
- "In the ever-evolving world of..."
- "As we navigate the complexities of..."
- "Let's dive in!"
- "Without further ado..."
- "Are you ready to take your X to the next level?"

**Hedging phrases:**
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to remember that..."
- "One might argue that..."
- "It goes without saying..."
- "Needless to say..."

**Empty transitions:**
- "That being said..."
- "With that in mind..."
- "Moving on to the next point..."
- "Now that we've covered X, let's look at Y..."
- "Speaking of which..."

**Generic closings:**
- "In conclusion..."
- "To sum up..."
- "To wrap things up..."
- "As we've seen..."
- "At the end of the day..."

**Buzzwords (flag if overused, not every instance):**
- leverage, synergy, cutting-edge, game-changer, revolutionize
- paradigm shift, holistic approach, best-in-class
- robust, seamless, scalable, innovative
- empower, unlock, unleash, supercharge

### Tone Inconsistency Patterns

- Sudden shift from casual to academic voice
- Paragraphs that sound like a different author wrote them
- Abrupt changes in reading level
- Sections that are much more verbose than surrounding text

## Tone Checks

### "We" Voice Enforcement

**Find and replace patterns:**
| Find | Replace With |
|------|-------------|
| "I think" | "We believe" |
| "I recommend" | "We recommend" |
| "In my experience" | "In our experience" |
| "I've found that" | "We've found that" |
| "My approach" | "Our approach" |
| "I suggest" | "We suggest" |

Also check for:
- "One should..." (too passive) → "We recommend..."
- "It is recommended..." (passive) → "We recommend..."
- "Users should..." (detached) → "You should..." or "We suggest..."
- "The company" (third person) → "We" (first person plural)

### Educational-Promotional Balance

The article should feel like:
- A knowledgeable friend explaining something at a whiteboard
- NOT a textbook (too dry)
- NOT a sales page (too pushy)
- NOT a blog post by an individual (too personal)

Every section should teach the reader something valuable. The promotional angle comes through expertise and helpfulness, not through selling.
